# Healthcare Go-to-Market Strategy

GTM for healthcare is fundamentally different from general B2B SaaS: relationship-driven, committee-governed, evidence-dependent, 12-18 month sales cycles.

## The Healthcare GTM Pipeline

```
Clinical Validation → KOL Endorsement → Lighthouse Pilots → VAC Approval → Enterprise Contract → Expansion
```

### Stage 1: Clinical Validation
- Generate evidence (studies, data, peer-reviewed publications)
- Secure FDA clearance/approval if applicable
- Build KOL advisory board (3-5 physicians)
- Achieve HIPAA compliance, begin SOC 2 / HITRUST

### Stage 2: Lighthouse Pilots
- Run 2-3 parallel pilots at reference health systems
- Budget 18 months of runway for full pilot-to-contract cycle
- Frame value across 3 dimensions: patient impact, clinical burden reduction, revenue/time ROI
- Engage clinicians early (5x effectiveness multiplier per Deloitte 2025)

### Stage 3: Champion Development
- Convert pilot champions into internal advocates
- Arm champions with VAC-ready evidence packages
- Build stakeholder maps for each target system
- Power to say "no" lives with legal, compliance, and clinical ops

### Stage 4: Enterprise Procurement
- Navigate VAC with separate materials per persona
- Prepare for 12-18 month procurement cycle
- Address EHR integration (Epic, Cerner, Meditech)
- Secure reimbursement codes if applicable

### Stage 5: Scale and Expand
- Post-market surveillance through real-world evidence
- Land-and-expand across departments/facilities
- Build case studies for next health system

## Buying Personas

| Persona | Cares About |
|---------|-------------|
| **CMIO** | Clinical workflow, EHR compatibility, evidence base |
| **CIO/CTO** | Architecture, security, scalability, integration |
| **CFO** | ROI, total cost of ownership, reimbursement impact |
| **Chief Innovation Officer** | Differentiation, patient experience |
| **Compliance Officer** | HIPAA, HITRUST, state regulations, BAA |
| **Nurse Manager** | Ease of use, workflow impact, training burden |
| **Value Analysis Committee** | Clinical evidence, cost, risk, vendor reliability |
| **Procurement** | Contract terms, vendor stability, TCO |

Average 9 people weigh in on a hospital tech purchase.

## Healthcare vs General SaaS GTM

| Dimension | General SaaS | Healthcare |
|-----------|-------------|------------|
| Sales cycle | 1-6 months | 12-18 months |
| Buying committee | 3-5 people | 9+ stakeholders |
| Compliance | SOC 2, maybe GDPR | HIPAA + SOC 2 + HITRUST |
| Pricing premium | Standard | 15-25% for compliance |
| Content risk | Low | One wrong medical term kills deal |
| Integration | API/webhooks | EHR integration (Epic, Cerner) |
| Validation | Customer testimonials | Clinical validation studies |
| Contract duration | Annual | 7-10 years enterprise |
| Self-serve/PLG | Common | Rare — trust-driven |

## Compliance as Sales Enabler

Stack compliance strategically — each tier opens new buyer segments:

| Compliance | Opens Access To | Timeline |
|-----------|-----------------|----------|
| HIPAA | Any healthcare vendor relationship | Immediate |
| SOC 2 Type II | Enterprise hospital procurement | 6-9 months |
| HITRUST e1/i1 | Large health systems, payers | 12-18 months |
| HITRUST r2 | Top-tier health systems, government | 24+ months |
| FDA clearance | Clinical claims, reimbursement codes | Varies |

## Channels That Work

### KOL Marketing
- Quality over quantity in KOL relationships (2025 trend)
- AI-powered KOL tiering by research output, conference presence, social reach
- Omnichannel: conference + virtual + social + peer-to-peer programs

### Medical Conferences (Declining)
- Attendance 35% below pre-pandemic levels
- Digital-first KOL strategies more cost-effective
- Conversations moved to threads, podcasts, virtual communities

### Clinical Validation
- Clinical evidence packages designed for VAC approval
- KOLs as early adopters who present data and author publications
- Peer validation through lighthouse health system references

### Digital
- LinkedIn targeting healthcare professionals with KOL content
- Email nurture with trust-building KOL content
- Healthcare podcasts and virtual communities

## Pricing Models

| Model | When to Use | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Per-Patient Per-Month** | RPM, population health | $40-80 PPPM |
| **Per-Provider/Seat** | Clinical SaaS, EHR | By provider count |
| **Transaction-Based** | RCM, billing, claims | Per claim/event |
| **Tiered Subscription** | Multi-segment | Solo → group → enterprise |
| **Outcome-Based** | Value-based care | Tied to measurable outcomes |
| **Enterprise License** | Large health systems | Capital + implementation |

Volume discounts: expect 15-25% when scaling beyond 250 beds.

## Pilot Best Practices

- Run 2-3 pilots in parallel (not sequential)
- Budget 18 months: pilot + procurement + deployment
- Every pitch answers 3 questions:
  1. What is the patient impact?
  2. What is the clinical burden reduction?
  3. What is the revenue or time ROI?
- Compress from 12-month saga to 3-month trial + 1-month expansion decision

## Anti-Patterns

- ❌ Selling features instead of outcomes
- ❌ Single-threaded champion dependency (champion leaves = deal dies)
- ❌ Skipping VAC preparation materials
- ❌ Wrong medical terminology in content (kills credibility)
- ❌ Assuming self-serve/PLG will work (healthcare is relationship-driven)
- ❌ Ignoring EHR integration requirements
- ❌ Launching sales before clinical validation
- ❌ Treating procurement as objective RFP process (it's relationship-driven)
