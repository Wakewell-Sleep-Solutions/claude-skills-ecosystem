# Professional Business Email — Dental Practice Operations

A dental practice CEO writes 20+ business emails weekly. This skill generates effective correspondence backed by industry data and success rate benchmarks.


**Step 0:** Read `.claude/context/practice-context.md` for practice overview, brand voice, patient pain points, and proof points before proceeding.
## 1. Insurance Appeals (54% overturn rate — yet <1% of denials are appealed)

**The biggest missed revenue opportunity in dental.** Properly documented appeals overturn 54.3% of denials. Administrative denials: 78% overturn. "Not medically necessary": ~70%. Prior auth denials: 65%. "Not covered": ~35%. Claim denial rates rose from 9% (2016) to 15% (2023). Rework cost per denial: $25-$50.

### Winning Appeal Structure
```
Subject: Appeal of Claim Denial — Patient: [Name], Claim #[Number]

Dear Claims Review Department,

I am writing to appeal denial of claim #[Number] for patient [Name]
(ID: [Insurance ID], DOB: [DOB]).

Denied procedure: [CDT Code] — [Description]
Denial reason stated: [Quote their exact reason]
Date of service: [Date]

CLINICAL JUSTIFICATION:
[NEW detailed narrative — not a copy of original notes. Connect clinical
findings directly to treatment rendered. Answer: What is the diagnosis?
What evidence supports it? Why is this the appropriate treatment?
What happens without it?]

SUPPORTING DOCUMENTATION ENCLOSED:
☐ Intraoral photographs (reduces denial by ~40% vs X-rays alone for cracked teeth)
☐ Periapical/panoramic radiographs
☐ Periodontal charting
☐ Clinical notes from date of service
☐ Peer-reviewed literature supporting approach

I request a dentist-to-dentist peer review with your dental consultant.
Please contact our office at [phone] if additional information is needed.

Sincerely,
Dr. [Name], [Credentials]
NPI: [Number]
```

### Appeal Escalation Path
1. First appeal: new detailed narrative + all supporting docs
2. Second appeal: even more detailed + additional evidence
3. Request dentist-to-dentist peer review
4. File complaint with state insurance commissioner
5. Engage ADA's third-party payer concierge

### 2026 CDT Update: 31 new codes, 12 revised, 6 deleted. Major carriers increasingly want original clinical notes over written narratives.

## 2. Specialist Referral Letters

### Required Elements (per HIPAA minimum necessary)
- Referring dentist name, address, phone, NPI
- Patient name, DOB, address, phone
- Urgency level
- Presenting complaint and history
- Clinical findings and relevant medical history
- Whether opinion or management is sought
- Relevant radiographs and diagnostic records

### HIPAA-Compliant Transmission
- **Unencrypted email with PHI = violation** regardless of intent
- Treatment/payment/operations disclosures permitted without patient authorization
- Options: encrypted email (Google Workspace/M365 with BAA), secure fax, HIPAA platforms (HIPAAtizer, Jotform Healthcare, NexHealth)
- Include only minimum necessary PHI
- Consumer Gmail/Outlook.com do NOT qualify — must be business tier with BAA

### Templates

**Sending referral:**
> Dear Dr. [Name],
> I am referring [Patient] for evaluation of [area/condition]. [2-3 sentence clinical summary]. Enclosed: [list attachments]. Please send treatment summary upon completion. Thank you for your excellent care.

**Referral thank you:**
> Dear Dr. [Name],
> Thank you for referring [Patient]. We have completed [general description]. The patient is doing well. We appreciate your confidence and look forward to continuing to serve your patients.

## 3. Vendor Negotiations (15-25% savings potential)

Supply costs consume up to 9% of revenue (vs 6% pre-pandemic). Price variations of 15-25% on identical products within the same week. Only 35% of dentists expressed economic optimism in early 2025.

### Negotiation Email Template
```
Subject: Supply Agreement Review — [Practice Name]

Dear [Rep],

Thank you for our [X]-year partnership. As we review agreements for [year],
I'd like to discuss pricing for [categories].

Our current volume: [X units/month]. I've received competitive quotes
at [X% lower] for comparable products. Before making any changes,
I want to explore whether we can adjust our current terms.

Specifically interested in:
- Volume-based pricing tiers
- Annual contract with locked pricing
- Bundled shipping/returns terms

Could we schedule a call this week?
```

### Strategies That Work
- **GPO membership** — collective buying power (Synergy Dental Partners, NDC)
- **Consolidate vendors** — some practices place 270+ orders/year; consolidation = leverage
- **Real-time pricing tools** — compare market prices at time of order
- **Total value focus** — shipping, returns, support, not just unit price
- **Documented savings: $24,000/year** through systematic supply management

## 4. Staff Communications

### Daily Huddle Template (15 min)
- Today's schedule overview + production goal
- Unconfirmed appointments / potential gaps
- Special patient notes (anxiety, VIP, new)
- Yesterday's wins / recognition
- Any operational issues

### Policy Update Memo
```
Subject: Updated [Policy] — Effective [Date]

Team,

Starting [date], we're implementing [change].

What's changing: [specific description]
Why: [brief reasoning]
What you need to do: [action items]
Timeline: [when]

Questions? Let's discuss at huddle on [day], or reach out to [contact].
```

### Performance Review Cadence
- New hires: monthly reviews for first 6 months
- Veterans: every 3-4 months
- Annual on work anniversary
- Include self-appraisals + two-way feedback
- Track weekly notes: attitude, punctuality, communication, clinical skills

## 6. DSO/Partnership Correspondence

**Market context:** US DSO market $26.9B (2023), 16.4% CAGR through 2030. IDSO model: sell 51-80% for cash, retain clinical autonomy.

### Letter of Intent Key Elements
- Purchase price and payment structure
- Assets included (equipment, records, goodwill)
- Seller transition/employment terms
- Non-compete and non-solicitation
- Due diligence period
- Confidentiality clause (often binding even if LOI is non-binding)
- Check existing management agreements, health plan contracts, lease assignments

## 7. Patient Complaint Response (HIPAA-critical)

**Real penalties:** $50,000 (NC practice), $10,000 (Elite Dental Associates) for PHI in review responses.

### De-Escalation Framework
1. **Acknowledge** concern without admitting fault
2. **Empathize** in general terms (never reference specific visit/treatment)
3. **Move offline** — provide phone/email for private discussion
4. **Document** internally with timestamps
5. **Follow up** within 24-48 hours
6. **Never** confirm or deny patient status in any public response

## 8. Dental Board Correspondence

### CE Audit Survival
- Digital-first documentation expected in 2026
- Maintain original certificates with name matching license exactly
- Organize by renewal period, store in 2+ locations
- Common deficiencies: incomplete units, missing mandatory courses (BLS, infection control, practice act, opioid prescribing)
- Most states: 30-day response window for audits
- Max 50% of CE units can be non-live/correspondence

### Complaint Response
- **Do not respond without legal counsel** — initial response sets evidentiary record
- Correct documentation gaps (CE hours, certifications) before they become issues

## Email Best Practices

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| Subject under 50 chars | Higher open rates |
| Under 200 words for requests | Respect recipient's time |
| Context → ask → next step | Every email has a clear CTA |
| Send Tue-Thu mornings | Highest open/response rates |
| Saturday for patient emails | 49% view rate, 5% CTR (peak) |
| Follow up at 5 business days | One follow-up, then try different channel |
| SMS > email for patients | 65-70% response vs 18-25% |
