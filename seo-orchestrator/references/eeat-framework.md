# E-E-A-T Evaluation Framework

Based on Google's Quality Rater Guidelines (September 2025 update).

## The Four Dimensions

### Experience
First-hand, life experience with the topic.
- **Signals:** Personal anecdotes, original photos/videos, detailed use-case descriptions, "I tested this" evidence
- **Red flags:** Generic advice with no personal angle, stock photos only, no indication the author has done the thing
- **Improve:** Add author bylines with bios, include first-person accounts, show real results/screenshots

### Expertise
Formal or informal knowledge and skill in the subject area.
- **Signals:** Credentials (degrees, certifications), professional background, depth of technical detail, accurate claims
- **Red flags:** Factual errors, superficial coverage, no author attribution, content outside the site's niche
- **Improve:** Display author credentials, cite primary sources, publish in-depth content, get expert review

### Authoritativeness
Reputation of the creator and the website within the field.
- **Signals:** Backlinks from authoritative sites, mentions in industry publications, awards, Wikipedia references, established domain history
- **Red flags:** No external mentions, brand-new domain with no reputation, contradicted by authoritative sources
- **Improve:** Earn editorial links, contribute to industry publications, build topical authority through comprehensive coverage

### Trustworthiness (MOST IMPORTANT)
Accuracy, honesty, safety, and reliability of the page and site.
- **Signals:** HTTPS, clear contact info, transparent authorship, accurate content, editorial policy, privacy policy, cited sources
- **Red flags:** Deceptive practices, hidden ads, missing contact info, user-generated spam, factual inaccuracies, no corrections policy
- **Improve:** Add trust signals (about page, contact page, editorial standards), fix inaccuracies promptly, disclose affiliations

## Audit Scoring Rubric

Rate each dimension 1-5 for content audits:

| Score | Label | Definition |
|-------|-------|------------|
| 5 | Excellent | Best-in-class; would be a reference source |
| 4 | Strong | Clear evidence; minor improvements possible |
| 3 | Adequate | Meets minimum bar; notable gaps |
| 2 | Weak | Significant deficiencies; risks ranking loss |
| 1 | Absent | No evidence; critical remediation needed |

### Scoring by Page Type
- **YMYL pages** (health, finance, legal, safety): Must score 4+ on all dimensions, 5 on Trustworthiness
- **Standard informational:** Must score 3+ across the board
- **Entertainment/casual:** Experience weighted more heavily; 2+ acceptable on Expertise

## YMYL Classification (Your Money or Your Life)
Topics where low-quality content could harm users:
- Health and medical information
- Financial advice and transactions
- Legal information
- News and current events (civic topics)
- Safety-related information
- Groups of people (potential for discrimination)

YMYL pages face the strictest E-E-A-T scrutiny. Always flag YMYL content for expert review.
