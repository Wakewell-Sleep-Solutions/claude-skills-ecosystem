# Sleep Practice Management

Covers both dental sleep medicine (oral appliance therapy) AND sleep testing centers (PSG/HSAT). These are different from general dental practice — they use medical billing, require physician partnerships, and follow AASM standards.

## 1. Sleep Dentistry (Oral Appliance Therapy)

### What It Is
Treating OSA and snoring with custom oral appliances (mandibular advancement devices). Dentist cannot independently diagnose sleep apnea — requires physician prescription based on qualifying sleep test.

### Billing Codes (Medical, NOT Dental)
| Code | Description | Avg Reimbursement |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **E0486** | Custom adjustable OA WITH fixed mechanical hinge | $1,700-$2,500 in-network |
| **K1027** | Custom adjustable OA WITHOUT fixed mechanical hinge | $1,700-$2,500 |

- Billed to medical insurance as DME (not dental insurance)
- All-inclusive lump sum: fabrication, fitting, adjustments, 90-day follow-up
- CMS confirmed for CY2026: oral appliances remain under Medicare DME benefit

### Medicare Requirements (LCD L33611)
- Face-to-face clinical evaluation by treating physician prior to sleep test
- Medicare-covered sleep test showing AHI or RDI ≥ 15
- Device provided and billed by licensed dentist (DDS or DMD)
- Practice must enroll as Medicare DME Supplier (CMS-855S)
- Requires surety bond + accreditation by approved body

### Case Acceptance Workflow
1. Screen (STOP-BANG, Epworth) → 2. Physician referral or internal screening
3. Sleep test (PSG or HSAT) → 4. Diagnosis by physician
5. Insurance verification → 6. Impressions/digital scan
7. Appliance fabrication → 8. Delivery + titration
9. Follow-up sleep test (efficacy) → 10. Ongoing compliance monitoring

## 2. Sleep Testing

### Study Types
| Type | CPT Code | Medicare Avg | Setting |
|------|----------|-------------|---------|
| Diagnostic PSG (no PAP) | 95810 | ~$625 | In-lab |
| Split-night PSG + CPAP | 95811 | Higher | In-lab |

- Insurance increasingly requires HST before approving in-lab PSG
- 67% of clinics report decreased PSG volume; 66% report increased HSAT
- All studies must be interpreted by board-certified sleep physician

## 3. Business Model

### Dual-Practice Advantage
Owner running both sleep testing + dental sleep medicine controls full lifecycle: screen → test → diagnose → treat → monitor compliance.

### Key Differences from General Dentistry
- **Medical billing** (not dental billing) — ICD-10 + CPT codes
- **DME supplier licensing** — required for Medicare billing
- **Physician partnerships** — medical director for interpretations
- **Different insurance** — medical insurance, not dental
- **DentalWriter Plus** — cross-codes dental→medical, SOAP narratives, 3,500+ dentists

### Revenue Model
- OAT case: $1,700-$2,500 in-network per appliance
- PSG study: ~$625 Medicare, higher commercial
- HSAT study: ~$169 Medicare, higher commercial
- Compliance follow-ups: recurring revenue

## 4. KPIs

### Dental Sleep Medicine
- New DSM patients/month (by source: physician referral vs DTC vs screening)
- Case acceptance rate (target 75-80%)
- Appliance deliveries/month (pipeline health)
- Compliance rate (≥5 nights/week, ≥4 hrs/night, 70% threshold)
- Revenue per case, claims denial rate
- Referral-to-consultation time (days)
- Physician referral volume (referrals/physician/month)

### Sleep Testing
- Studies/month (PSG + HSAT volume)
- HST completion rate (% producing scoreable data)
- Interpretation turnaround (days to physician-signed report)
- AHI positive rate (screening quality indicator)
- Conversion to treatment (% diagnosed → initiate CPAP or OAT)
- Device loss/damage rate (for reusable HST equipment)

## 6. Compliance

### AASM Accreditation (Jan 2025 Standards)
- Service-based model: Sleep Clinic, In-Lab Testing, HSAT, DME Supplier
- Category I (critical) and Category II (important) — both required
- Must follow all STRONG recommendation statements in active AASM CPGs
- State health facility license required for sleep centers

### Regulatory
- Board-certified sleep physician required for study interpretation
- DME supplier licensing varies by state
- Dental board regulations on OA scope of practice vary by state
- For dental offices offering HSAT: dentist orders test, physician interprets

### HIPAA & Sleep Data
- Sleep testing data (PSG, HSAT, AHI reports) = standard PHI
- Once wearable sleep data enters clinical setting → PHI under HIPAA
- 19 states classify consumer health data (including sleep metrics) as sensitive
- Health Information Privacy Reform Act (S.3097, Nov 2025) proposes extending coverage to wearables
